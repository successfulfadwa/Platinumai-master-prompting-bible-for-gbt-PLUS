📖 How to use this bible

👁️ Built for visual brains
Every section pairs text + diagram on purpose. Visual learning research (Paivio's dual-coding theory) shows ADHD brains retain up to 4× more when concepts are encoded in both verbal and visual channels. Look at the diagrams — they isolate the principle better than a photo could.
★ The core principle
Your prompts should stop sounding like "make it cool, realistic, luxury, 8K, cinematic."

They should sound like a physically plausible product photograph of a specific object, with defined material behavior, controlled studio lighting, precise camera language, locked composition, and clear constraints.

That is the difference between beginner prompting and senior art-direction.

01
Mental model

🧠 How the system reads your prompt

TL;DR
Write prompts as if a human production team will read them. Clear hierarchy beats secret wording. The system maps your words to visual patterns — concrete nouns + production-brief language wins every time.

Bad vs. better in one example

❌ Bad prompt
Make it realistic, cinematic, 8K, amazing, luxury, viral, photorealistic, beautiful, cool.
✓ Better prompt 
A photorealistic luxury product hero shot of a translucent platinum crystal logo on matte black onyx. Centered composition, 85mm macro lens, shallow depth of field, soft rectangular key light from upper left, subtle rim light from behind, realistic contact shadow, sharp faceted edges, internal mineral veining, no glow, no neon, no extra text.

High-weight prompt areas

Prompt areaWhy it matters
First sentenceEstablishes the main object/scene. Everything anchors here.
Concrete nouns"Brushed titanium," "matte onyx," "wet asphalt" > "premium."
Camera + lightControls realism and composition more than any style word.
Material wordsPrevents generic AI smoothness.
Preserve listCritical for edits — locks what shouldn't change.
Exact textPut your text in quotes. Helps the model render it cleanly.

Words that confuse the model

Confusing phraseWhy it fails
"Minimalist maximalist"Opposing art directions.
"Real photo but 3D render"Medium conflict unless clarified.
"Chrome but matte"Chrome is reflective; matte is diffuse.
"Candid luxury studio"Candid and studio conflict.
"Exact logo but redesign"Preserve vs. alter conflict.
◆ Hidden trick
Use role-like context inside the prompt:

"Treat this as a premium product photography brief for a luxury AI hardware brand."

This tells the model what decisions to optimize: taste, lighting, restraint, realism.
▲ Common mistake
Beginners describe vibe but not physics. "Luxury" isn't enough. Say what surface, what light, what camera, what material response, what composition.

Example prompts

Prompt 1 · Luxury object
Create a photorealistic luxury product hero shot of a four-point diamond crystal logo carved from translucent platinum-tinted mineral. Centered on matte black onyx, 85mm macro lens, shallow depth of field, soft rectangular key light from upper left, thin rim light on the rear edges, realistic contact shadow, razor-sharp facets, internal mineral veining, tiny micro-fractures, no glow, no neon, no text.
Prompt 2 · Realistic human
Create a natural photorealistic portrait of a woman in soft north-facing window light. 50mm lens, eye-level composition, visible pores, fine peach fuzz, slight skin redness around cheeks and nose, natural asymmetry, realistic eyebrow hairs, moist catchlights in the eyes, unretouched editorial photography, no plastic skin, no beauty-filter smoothing.
Prompt 3 · Exact edit
Edit only the word on the skin. Replace the existing word with "PLATINUM" in chrome-like raised letterforms. Preserve the exact face, pose, skin tone, lighting, camera angle, background, and expression. The chrome letters should look physically embedded into the skin with slight redness and pressure marks around the edges. Do not change anything else.

◆ Hidden trick · Reflective materials
For reflective materials, describe the reflection shape, not just the object.

Instead of: chrome logo

◆ Hidden trick · Camera distance
Specify camera distance, not just lens:

"85mm lens, tight head-and-shoulders portrait, camera 1.5 meters from subject"

This reduces full-body drift.
▲ Common mistake
Using "wide angle" when you want a flattering portrait. Wide lenses exaggerate faces. Use 50mm or 85mm for portraits.

04
Kill the plastic AI look

👁️ Skin, eyes, eyebrows, hair

TL;DR
Describe microstructure, color variation, asymmetry, and light interaction. Specify where the detail appears (cheeks, nose, neck, hands) — not just "realistic skin."

🆚 Plastic AI vs realistic — what changes

◆ Two faces · same prompt structure · different vocabulary
❌ PLASTIC AI
No pores · no asymmetry · perfect smooth skin · airbrushed sheen. Reads "AI" instantly.
✓ REALISTIC
Visible pores · slight redness on cheeks · asymmetric eyes · catchlights · individual brow hairs. Reads "real photo."
The right side has the same face — but with "visible pores, slight redness around nose, individual eyebrow hairs, catchlights, natural asymmetry" in the prompt.

📍 Where to add detail on a face

◆ Don't say "realistic skin" — name the zones
Each zone = a different vocabulary. Name 2-3 zones in your prompt instead of generic "realistic skin."

◆ Hidden trick · Anti-AI sheen
Use both negative + positive framing:

Negative: "not a beauty retouch, not a skincare ad, not airbrushed"
Positive: "realistic pores, natural unevenness, honest documentary texture"
▲ Common mistake
Only saying "realistic skin." Model may still give smooth influencer skin. Say where: cheeks, nose, forehead, neck, hands.

A close-up photorealistic portrait of a woman in soft window light. Visible pores on cheeks and nose, fine peach fuzz along jawline, slight redness around nostrils, natural under-eye texture, realistic eyebrow hairs, moist eyes with small catchlights, unretouched 50mm portrait, no airbrushing.
Prompt 2 · Eye detail
Extreme close-up of a human eye in natural daylight. Sharp iris fibers, wet cornea, realistic tear line, tiny blood vessels near the sclera, soft eyelashes crossing the frame, skin pores around eyelid, 100mm macro lens, shallow depth of field, no fantasy glow.
Prompt 3 · Chrome skin lettering
Photorealistic close-up of the word "PLATINUM" formed as raised chrome letters emerging from human skin. The skin remains realistic with pores, fine hairs, slight redness and pressure marks around each letter, chrome reflections follow the curved skin surface, macro lens, soft medical studio lighting.

For your robotics division

🤖 3D render + robots / mecha

TL;DR
For 3D and robots, use hard-surface language, plausible engineering, and material layering. Functional engineering beats glowing magic every time.

🆚 The crystal head trap — what you'll get vs what you want

◆ The single difference: skull VOLUME vs flat MASK
❌ FLAT MASK / HELMET
"Robot with diamond head" → gets you a helmet or visor with logo stuck on front. Flat. Reads cheap.
✓ SKULL VOLUME
"The head IS the diamond, full human-scale skull volume, no face, no visor" → you get the real concept.
Magic words: "full human-head scale, solid skull volume, not a flat mask, not a thin logo plate, no face, no visor, the entire head silhouette IS the diamond geometry"

For robotic heads, specify skull volume:
